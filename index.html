Md. Shadman Abid

Bio

Md. Shadman Abid is s currently employed as a research assistant at the Nanotechnology Research Center of Sultan Qaboos University (SQU), where he is engaged in advanced projects involving deep learning-based GIS mapping, image metadata extraction, and solar potential assessment using satellite imagery for energy networks. He previously worked at the Sustainable Energy Research Center at SQU, where he contributed to projects focused on optimum electric vehicle charging infrastructure, including techno-economic and environmental assessments of low-inertia microgrids. Shadman additionally served as a part-time lecturer in the Department of Electrical and Electronics Engineering (EEE) at Sonargaon University, Dhaka, Bangladesh. He graduated in 2022 with a B.Sc. degree in EEE from the Islamic University of Technology, Gazipur, Bangladesh. His research primarily focuses on deep learning, reinforcement learning, and robust optimization techniques for power system planning and policy, with a strong emphasis on modeling uncertainty in large-scale networks. His interests include advanced computational methods, uncertainty modeling of large-scale grid infrastructure, electric vehicle charging infrastructure planning, renewable energy integration, and smart distribution systems.

Current Projects

Data-driven Robust Optimization-based Model for Charging Infrastructure Allocation in EV-integrated Microgrids

  • Developed a two-stage Principal Component Data-Driven Robust Optimization-based Counter-factual Multi-Agent planning framework. This innovative model integrates renewable energy resources (RERs), battery energy storage systems (BES), BES-based virtual synchronous generators (VSG), and electric vehicle charging stations (EVCS) within microgrids, optimizing the allocation while addressing multiple stochastic variables and reducing computational load.
  • Aimed to enhance the average state of charge (SOC) for electric vehicles while simultaneously addressing the technological, economic, and environmental aspects of microgrid management. The proposed framework demonstrates superior performance in solution accuracy and computational efficiency compared to four contemporary methods, effectively balancing resource allocation with operational demands.
  • The work is currently under review.

Neural Architecture Search using Reinforcement Learning for Solar Metadata Prediction from Satellite Imagery

  • Developed a novel Neural Architecture Search (NAS) method utilizing a TD3-based reinforcement learning approach to predict critical solar metadata from satellite imagery. This method focuses on optimizing convolutional neural networks (CNNs) for robust extraction of solar data, addressing the high computational demands and dynamic challenges typical of conventional NAS.
  • The project aims to improve the accuracy of predicting solar power ratings, tilt angles, and azimuth values essential for optimizing solar energy use in microgrids. The project tested the novel NAS framework on high-resolution satellite images, demonstrating superior performance in prediction accuracy and computational efficiency compared to traditional NAS methods.
  • The work is currently under review.

Deep Learning-based GIS Mapping of Energy Networks

  • Currently developing an advanced deep learning framework using geospatial data to train models for the precise mapping of distribution grid components, including cables, conductors, poles, and transformers, and employed convolutional neural networks (CNNs) and recurrent neural networks (RNNs) to capture spatial and sequential patterns within extensive geospatial datasets, enhancing identification and categorization processes.
  • The project aims to provide a comprehensive and accurate representation of practical grid infrastructure for improved maintenance, planning, and expansion. The project focuses on increasing the resilience and efficiency of distribution grid operations by addressing dynamic grid configurations and the need for frequent infrastructure modification.

Robust Deep Learning for Spatiotemporal Forecasting of Renewable Energy Sources

  • Developed a Robust Deep Learning model to forecast renewable energy production from solar and wind sources. The model integrates correntropy-based training criterion for enhanced robustness and significantly improves the model's resistance to various sources of data contamination.
  • The project aimed to deliver accurate and reliable spatiotemporal forecasts of renewable energy production, addressing the challenge of power output variability across different times and locations. By comparing the RC-LSTM model's performance with ten state-of-the-art algorithms using contaminated datasets, the model demonstrated a superior ability to capture the fundamental dynamics of power production, thereby supporting better decision-making for utility operators and investors.
  • The work is currently under review.

Hierarchical Distributed ADMM for Electric Vehicle Charging Infrastructure

  • Developed a novel hierarchical distributed Alternating Direction Method of Multipliers (HD-ADMM) combined with a gradient-free ADMM-based Long Short-Term Memory (ADMM-LSTM) model. This approach streamlines the EV charging scheduling by predicting demand using ADMM-LSTM and optimizing charging strategies via HD-ADMM. The model efficiently handles the non-linearity and stochastic nature of EV charging, reducing dependency on gradient-based methods and numerical solvers.
  • This study aimed to enhance grid performance by reducing communication costs and improving convergence speed in EV charging infrastructure management. The dual-phase model was validated on a Norwegian operational network, demonstrating its capability to provide faster convergence, efficiently address grid and economic considerations for EV customers, and significantly reduce computational burden.
  • The work is currently under review.
